Essential Particles

Quick reference guide for the 13 most common Japanese particles (助詞).

✨ Tap any particle or example sentence to hear the pronunciation!

Core Markers

は
Topic (wa)
Introduces the main topic of the sentence.
これは本です。 As for this, it is a book.
が
Subject (ga)
Focuses on the specific subject/actor.
雨が降っています。 It is raining. (Focus on 'rain')
を
Direct Object (o)
Marks the direct object of a verb.
水を飲みます。 I drink water.
に
Destination/Time (ni)
Marks target, destination, or specific time.
日本に行きます。 I am going to Japan.
で
Location/Method (de)
Where an action happens or the tool used.
バスで学校へ行きます。 I go to school by bus.
も
Too / Also (mo)
Indicates addition (replaces wa, ga, o).
私も学生です。 I am also a student.

Logic & Connection

から
From / Because (kara)
Marks start point or states a reason.
忙しいから行けません。 Because I'm busy, I can't go.
ので
Because / Since (node)
Logical reason (more polite than kara).
雨なので遅れました。 Since it was raining, I was late.
だけ
Only / Just (dake)
Sets a limit to the quantity or scope.
日本語だけ話せます。 I only speak Japanese.
て
Connector (te)
Te-form used to link actions together.
食べて寝ます。 I eat and (then) sleep.

Questions & Nuance

か
Question / Or (ka)
Marks a question or provides an alternative.
本か雑誌を買います。 I buy a book or a magazine.
ね
Agreement (ne)
Seeking agreement. Like "..., isn't it?".
いい天気ですね。 Nice weather, isn't it?
よ
Emphasis (yo)
Asserting new info or strong opinion.
明日休みだよ！ Tomorrow is a holiday (I'm tellin' ya)!
