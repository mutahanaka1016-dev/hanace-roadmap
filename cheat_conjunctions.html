🟢 Beginner Level (JLPT N5–N4)

Focus: Daily conversation and basic sentence linking.
Japanese English Meaning Usage Note
だからSo / ThereforeUsed in casual speech to show a result.
それでAnd then / SoUsed to lead into the next part of a story.
でもBut / HoweverThe most common way to show contrast in conversation.
しかしBut / HoweverMore formal than "Demo"; common in writing/polite speech.
そしてAnd / ThenUsed to list actions or items in sequence.
それからAnd then / After thatUsed when one action follows another.
それにBesides / MoreoverUsed to add more information or reasons.
またはOrUsed to give choices (Formal).
それともOrUsed specifically in questions (A or B?).

🟡 Intermediate Level (JLPT N3–N2)

Focus: Logical flow, explanations, and smoother transitions.
Japanese English Meaning Usage Note
そこでBecause of thatUsed when a situation leads someone to take action.
するとThereupon / ThenUsed when one action causes an immediate, natural result.
したがってAccordingly / ThereforeVery logical and formal; used in reports or speeches.
ところがHowever / Even soUsed when the result is unexpected or surprising.
それなのにDespite thatOften carries a feeling of "it shouldn't be this way."
つまりIn other wordsUsed to summarize or rephrase something simply.
なぜならBecause...Used to introduce a reason (usually ends with ~kara da).
例たとえばFor exampleUsed to provide specific instances.
ところでBy the wayUsed to change the topic completely.
さてWell / NowUsed to transition to the next main point or task.

🔴 Advanced Level (JLPT N1 / Business)

Focus: Formal writing, academic papers, and professional settings.
Japanese English Meaning Usage Note
ゆえにConsequently / ThereforeHighly academic or literary; rarely used in speech.
よってTherefore / HenceCommon in formal announcements, certificates, or logic.
ただしProvided that / HoweverUsed to add a condition or an exception to a rule.
もっともAlthough / ThoughUsed to add a nuanced correction to a previous statement.
およびAnd / As well asUsed in legal or very formal business documents.
かつAnd / At the same timeUsed to describe two simultaneous qualities or states.
さらにはFurthermoreAdds an even stronger point than the previous ones.
すなわちNamely / That isUsed to define something more precisely.
いわばSo to speakUsed when using a metaphor or analogy.
