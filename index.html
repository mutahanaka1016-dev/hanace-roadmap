Hi there! I’m Hanaka, the founder and lead coach of Hana Banana Japanese.

When I first started teaching Japanese, I noticed a common hurdle that almost every student faced: Japanese is complex, and it’s incredibly easy to get lost along the way. Traditional textbooks often leave learners feeling overwhelmed, struggling to connect grammar formulas to real, heartfelt conversations.

That is why I created Hanace (ハナス).

Hanace is a name born from two words very close to my heart: Hana-Trace (tracing your path to bloom) and Hanasu (to speak 🗣️).

I wanted to create more than just a study tool. I wanted to give you a clear, fun roadmap that builds your confidence step-by-step, so you can stop worrying about mistakes and start laughing, sharing, and bonding with Japanese people in real life.

Your Japanese doesn't have to be perfect to be beautiful. Let’s trace your progress, build that courage, and get you talking!

💡 Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much time should I study every day?

A: Aim for 15 to 30 minutes every single day. Consistency is much more powerful than cramming for hours once a week.

Q: I keep forgetting vocabulary. What should I do?

A: Everyone forgets! The key is Spaced Repetition. Review the vocabulary lists using flashcard apps like Anki, or try making your own bizarre or funny sentences with the new words!

Q: Do I really need to learn Kanji?

A: While Hiragana and Katakana are the foundation, Kanji is essential for reading anything in Japan. Start slowly with the most visual Kanji and you will naturally start recognizing patterns.
